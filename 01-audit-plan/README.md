This section outlines the strategic planning phase of an internal cybersecurity audit. A clear plan ensures alignment with business objectives, risk priorities, and NIST audit methodology.

## Audit Scope

- Environment: Internal systems (e.g., corporate network, identity management, endpoint protection)
- Exclusions: External vendor assessments, customer environments
- Timeline: 3-week audit cycle
- Stakeholders: IT, Security, Governance, Internal Audit, Business Unit Leaders

## Audit Objectives

- Validate alignment with NIST CSF and internal policy
- Identify control gaps, misconfigurations, or risk exposures
- Evaluate documentation, SOPs, and technical controls
- Recommend improvements tied to business goals

## Methodology

The audit will follow a structured methodology based on:

- NIST CSF (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover)
- ISO 19011 for audit principles
- Risk-based prioritization
- Interviews, evidence review, and control walkthroughs

## Deliverables

- Audit plan document (PDF or Markdown)
- Stakeholder notification template
- Risk-based control testing plan
- Kickoff meeting agenda
