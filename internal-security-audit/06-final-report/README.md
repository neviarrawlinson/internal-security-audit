The final audit report synthesizes findings, control effectiveness, risk exposure, and actionable recommendations. It serves as the primary deliverable to stakeholders.

## Objectives

- Document key audit findings aligned to NIST CSF
- Communicate risks, gaps, and remediation priorities
- Provide actionable guidance for control improvements
- Create a formal record of the audit process

## Report Structure

1. **Executive Summary**
   - Overview of scope, timeline, objectives, and key findings

2. **Methodology**
   - Reference to NIST CSF, ISO 19011, and evidence collection approach

3. **Control Effectiveness Summary**
   - Per NIST function: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover

4. **Key Findings**
   - Gaps or nonconformities with evidence and impact

5. **Risk Ratings**
   - High, Medium, Low (with rationale and affected areas)

6. **Recommendations**
   - Prioritized remediation actions, ownership, and timeframes

7. **Appendices**
   - Control mapping matrix, evidence binder summary, references

## Best Practices

- Keep the tone professional and executive-ready
- Use visual indicators (e.g., tables or charts if exporting to PDF)
- Link to audit artifacts and supporting evidence
- Align to business priorities and compliance objectives
